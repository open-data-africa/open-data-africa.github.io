Guides & Sources of Open Datasets

Discover where to find valuable open datasets across government portals, research institutions, civic tech, and international initiatives. These resources support African innovators, researchers, and policymakers.

Government Portals

National statistics offices and ministries often publish public datasets on agriculture, health, and education.

  • Kenya Open Data
  • Nigeria Open Data
  • South Africa National Data Portal
  • Ethiopia ESS
  • Ghana Statistical Service

International Platforms

Sources like World Bank, WHO, and UN offer global and regional open datasets useful for Africa-focused projects.

  • World Bank Data
  • UN Data
  • OECD Data
  • WHO Global Health Observatory
  • UNICEF Data

Research Institutions

Universities and think tanks frequently release data from fieldwork, surveys, or experiments in African contexts.

  • DataFirst (UCT South Africa)
  • Harvard Dataverse
  • ICRISAT Dataverse
  • ILRI Dataverse (Livestock)

Open Data Directories

Platforms like CKAN, DataHub, and African Open Science Framework provide searchable dataset registries.

  • CKAN Dataset Catalogs
  • DataHub.io
  • OpenAFRICA
  • Open Data Inventory (ODIN)
  • FAIRsharing.org

Civic Tech & NGOs

Non-profits and civic tech communities publish datasets on governance, health, and transparency.

  • Code for Africa
  • Open Knowledge Foundation
  • Data Science Africa
  • Digital Earth Africa
  • GODAN – Global Open Data for Agriculture & Nutrition

International Cooperation

These initiatives support open data, digital public infrastructure, and responsible AI through global partnerships.

  • FAIR Forward (GIZ)
  • Global Partnership for Sustainable Development Data
  • PARIS21 (Partnership in Statistics for Development)

Open Data Africa

Open data ecosystems across Africa through benchmarking, recognition, and capacity building.
