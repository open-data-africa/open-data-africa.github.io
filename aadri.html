Overview

The Africa AI Dataset Readiness Index (ADRI) is a continental benchmarking framework designed to evaluate datasets based on their readiness for AI, openness, governance, sustainability, and socio-economic impact.

Evaluation Pillars

AI Readiness

Machine-readable, structured, and usable for AI models.

Openness

Accessible and openly licensed for reuse.

Governance & FAIR

Ethical, documented, and aligned with FAIR principles.

Socio-Economic Impact

Real-world applications and measurable benefits.

Sustainability

Maintained, updated, and institutionally supported.

Scoring Methodology

Each dataset is scored across five pillars using a 1–5 scale. The final ADRI score is calculated using a weighted formula.

ADRI Score = [(AI × 30%) + (O × 20%) + (G × 20%) + (SE × 15%) + (S × 15%)] × 20

Classification Tiers

  • Platinum: 90–100
  • Gold: 80–89
  • Silver: 70–79
  • Bronze: 60–69
  • Below 60: Needs Improvement

Eligibility Criteria

  • Africa-relevant dataset
  • Machine-readable format
  • Publicly accessible or managed access
  • Updated within the last 3 years
  • Clear metadata
